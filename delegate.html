The problem

You see something.
You want it handled.
And then... life happens.

Every day, you come across things that should be delegated. A competitor's pricing page. A LinkedIn profile worth reaching out to. An article your team should read. An email that needs a response.

What you used to do:
01 Open a new tab to write a Slack message or email
02 Copy the URL. Take a screenshot. Start explaining.
03 Lose context. Re-open the original page. Start over.
04 Postpone it. You'll send it later. You're busy right now.
05 Forget it entirely. Or worse — do it yourself at 11pm.
"The delay between deciding to delegate and actually delegating is where productivity goes to die."
The insight

Delegation dies in
the gap between
intent and action.

You're in a meeting. Someone mentions a company you should research. You think: someone should look into this. But the moment passes. The meeting moves on. By the time it's over, you've forgotten.

You're browsing and spot a brilliant article your team should read. You think: I'll forward this later. Later never comes.

Delegate closes that gap. The moment you think it — it's delegated. No tab switching. No context loss. No forgetting.
