How the Money Was Made

High agency.
Zero handholding.

The results didn't come from a playbook. There was no content brief, no keyword architect, no tech support. What existed was a bit of a shaky SEO foundation, redundant URLs, and a blank slate. What emerged was a fully owned revenue channel.

01
Optimised What Existed — Without Any Support
Inherited a library of underperforming blogs sitting on a shaky structural SEO base. Rather than starting fresh, went deep — rewrote for ergonomics, reduced cognitive load in UX and visual content design, and made every existing blog work harder for conversion. No tech team. No designer. No direction. Just a clear-eyed understanding of what was broken and the instinct to fix it.
02
Picked the Right Topics — Before Anyone Else Did
Owned the entire research-to-publish pipeline: identifying keyword opportunities that should have been written two years ago, placing calculated bets, writing, and measuring. No content briefs. No editorial oversight. No architect. This is what senior-level content strategy looks like — not waiting for assignments, but knowing which bets to take.
03
Flagged 120+ Redundant URLs — The Bold Call
While others kept writing more, took a step back and audited the entire blog architecture. Identified 120+ thin and redundant URLs actively diluting Wishup's topical authority. This caused initial panic — but Raymond confirmed it, Koray's framework confirmed it, and fixing it is now a known priority. This is not what a writer does. This is what a strategist does.
04
Generated Results on a Shaky Foundation
All of this — every lead, every conversion — happened on top of a shaky SEO setup. 29 conversions with the wind against you is a different kind of result than with a clean, optimised architecture. Imagine what happens when the foundation and blog UI is fixed.
High Agency
Owned the entire research, architecture, writing, and lead generation without waiting for instructions or asking for permission to do more.
